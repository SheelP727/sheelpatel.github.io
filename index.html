Sheel Patel is a Ben Franklin Scholar at the University of Pennsylvania and an undergraduate in Health and Societies with a concentration in Healthcare Markets and Finance, plus Nutrition Science and a minor in Chemistry. His work sits at the intersection of strategy, medtech, and clinical research, with a particular interest in pediatric hypertension, kidney disease, and how digital tools can support proactive, equitable care.

Current Projects

  • Pediatric hypertension and ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM) - Clinical Research Assistant in the CHOP Department of Nephrology, extracting and analyzing ABPM data and EPIC charts for more than 500 pediatric patients to identify risk factors for white coat hypertension and elevated blood pressure.
  • Vitality Labs - digital health and habit formation - Co-founding a wellness platform that integrates wearables, behavioral nudging, and data visualization to make long term habit change measurable and actionable.
  • Our Beautiful Earth - youth environmental advocacy - Building a digital hub that has reached more than 2.5K users across eight countries, focused on climate justice, actionable sustainability, and youth led organizing.
  • Global and community health work - Preparing for Penn GRIP research in Monteverde, Costa Rica and reflecting on clinical shadowing through the Atlantis Fellowship in Lisbon to compare care delivery across health systems.
