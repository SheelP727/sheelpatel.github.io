About

I am an undergraduate at the University of Pennsylvania and a Ben Franklin Scholar in the College of Arts & Sciences. I study Health and Societies with a concentration in Healthcare Markets and Finance, along with Nutrition Science and a Chemistry minor. My work lives at the intersection of strategy, medtech, and clinical research, with a particular interest in pediatric hypertension, kidney disease, and how digital tools can support proactive, equitable care.

I split my time between the clinic and the whiteboard: analyzing ambulatory blood pressure data at CHOP, building digital health products through Vitality Labs, and working with student consulting teams on questions of adoption, market entry, and system design. Long term, I hope to train as a physician while shaping the infrastructure that makes high-quality care sustainable and accessible.
